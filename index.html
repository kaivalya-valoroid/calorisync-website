Questions, answered

Is my health data private?

Yes. Your food logs and health data are stored on encrypted servers in India. We never sell your data, and you can export or delete everything at any time from inside the app.

Will there be an Android app?

Yes — we're working on it. CaloriSync launched on iPhone first so we could go deep on Apple Health integration and voice logging. Android is on the roadmap. Drop us a line at support@calorisync.com and we'll let you know when it ships.

How is this different from MyFitnessPal or HealthifyMe?

CaloriSync is built for the way Indians actually eat. The IFCT-2017 food database, Indian portion sizes (katori, roti, plate, glass), bilingual voice logging, and Kai — an AI coach that adapts to your goals — are all designed around real Indian kitchens.

Is Kai a doctor?

No. Kai is an AI coach that gives general dietary suggestions. It is not medical advice. If you have a medical condition, allergies, or are pregnant, please consult a qualified doctor or registered dietitian before changing your diet.

Can I cancel anytime?

Yes. Subscriptions are managed by Apple — cancel from Settings → your Apple ID → Subscriptions, anytime. Your trial doesn't auto-charge if you cancel within 7 days.

Does it work offline?

Logging meals you've eaten before and viewing your history works offline. AI food scanning and Kai chat need an internet connection because they run on cloud-hosted AI models.

Where is my data stored?

On encrypted servers in India. AI food scans and Kai chat messages are processed through enterprise AI partners (currently Google Gemini) under a Data Processing Agreement — they don't keep your data or use it to train their models.
